Learning About the Grieving Process

While reading it is important to know that while these stages and steps are the most easily recognizable and taught people usually don’t follow the process exactly as listed. Each person is di fferent, and as such they could go through these stages much di fferently, go though the order di fferently, or skip some stages altogether. It’s important to learn about these stages so you can identify any warning signs, but it is also equally important to recognize when the person themselves are showing their own signs

Depression

Many Americans throughout the United States su ffer from Major Depressive Disorder, and the most common symptoms are:

  • Feeling of tearfulness, emptiness, or hopelessness
  • Outbursts of anger, irritable, or frustrated over small things/problems
  • Lost of interests in normal activities
  • Insomnia or sleeping in excess
  • Tiredness and lack of energy
  • Changes in appetite, loss of appetite or increased craving
  • Can lead to weight gain or weight loss
  • Restlessness, anxiety, agitation
  • Slow thinking, movement, speech
  • Feeling of guilt, worthlessness, thinking of past failures, and blaming things that on yourself that weren’t your responsibility
  • Trouble thinking, remembering things, concentrating, making decisions
  • Frequent/recurrent thoughts of death, suicidal thoughts, attempts at suicide
  • Unexplained physical pains like back pain and or headaches
